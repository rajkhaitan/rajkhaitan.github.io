Project: Printed Circuit Board

Course: Digital Electronics

Duration: 2 weeks

Objective: To take an idea and make it into a circuit that will become a printed ciruit board

Type: Individual Project (1 person)

Description: For this project, we had 2 weeks to pick a circuit that we woould first build in a CDS, then breadboard and eventually print onto a circuit baord. As my circuit, I decided to build a low battery indicator. In this circuit, an LED would have a solid light if the power coming in was below a certain voltage, in this case 5 volts. If it was above the voltage level, then the LED would start to flash. The goal was to have a completely working printed circuit board at then end of the 2 weeks. To first build the circuit, I first used Multisim which is circuit design software. Once completed, I transferred my circuit onto a breadboard which utilized an LED, a few resistors, a capacitor and a power source for which I used a battery. Once I got my breadboard to work, I built it as a PCB in Fritzing, another software for designing circuits. This circuit was printed as a PCB on glossy paper. We also had to make a circuit board from scratch which meant we started out a piece of copper. The printed circuit was ironed onto the copper which was then etched in Ferric Chloride to take away the copper except from the connections between all the different components. Throughout this, I documented the entire process and the document can be accesed here. Unfortunately, at the end of this project, my circuit was not working. What I realized is that I might have confused myself when printing out the circuit and soldering the components as some of them might have been flipped.


Project: AOI Circuit Design

Course: Digital Electronics

Duration: 2 weeks

Objective: To create and design a circuit of your idea that helps solve a sort of problem

Type: Individual Project (1 person)

Description: For this project, we had 2 weeks to create any idea of our choice and using AOI logic, create a completely working circuit that models what we thought of making. The goal of this project was to create a circuit that would ideally solve a problem as this was supposed to be a solution to that. Since I was working individually on this project, I only needed to have four inputs. The idea that I had was a pass or fail circuit that based on the inputs of homework, behavior, participation and tests would tell you if you were able to pass or fail a class. After coming up with my idea, I first needed to create a truth table that would give me all the possible scenarios in which the output would be a 1 showing that the student could pass the class. After this, I simplified my Logic Expression so that it was simple enough to be created on Multisim, a circuit design software. After testing this circuit, I then breadboarded this. My circuit was working exactly how I intended for it to work. Along with the project, I had to document the entire process which showed all the details and more images of what I was creating. This document can be accesed here.
